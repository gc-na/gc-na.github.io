# Chase Na (나경채, 羅敬埰)

**Chase Na** (Korean: 나경채, Chinese: 羅敬埰) is a South Korean VLSI engineer and the founder of VLSI Wiki, an open community dedicated to semiconductor and VLSI knowledge sharing. He is known for his contributions to semiconductor education and his advocacy for advancing the global semiconductor industry.

## Early life and education

Chase Na was born in South Korea. He was greatly influenced by his father, who worked at Samsung Electronics, which sparked his early interest in electronics and semiconductor technology. His curiosity about how electronic devices work, combined with his family background in the semiconductor industry, led him to pursue a career in VLSI (Very Large Scale Integration) engineering.

## Career

Na currently works as a Prime* Product Engineer at Synopsys, specializing in VLSI design and semiconductor technology. Throughout his career, he has worked on various aspects of chip design, including:

- **Digital Circuit Design**: Expertise in ASIC and SoC design methodologies
- **Physical Implementation**: Experience in placement, routing, and timing closure
- **EDA Tools**: Proficiency in electronic design automation software
- **Design for Test (DFT)**: Implementation of testability features in semiconductor designs

His professional focus includes advancing semiconductor technologies that enable future innovations in artificial intelligence, autonomous driving, Mars exploration, and the Metaverse.

## VLSI Wiki

### Foundation and purpose

In his commitment to knowledge sharing, Na founded **VLSI Wiki**, an open community platform that encompasses all knowledge related to semiconductors and VLSI technology. The platform serves as a comprehensive resource covering:

- Fundamental semiconductor theories
- Latest technology trends in VLSI
- Practical engineering insights
- Career guidance for VLSI professionals

### Mission statement

Na's stated mission is to "serve as a beacon of progress for the global semiconductor industry, sparking positive change for both companies and society at large." He believes that semiconductors are the essential hardware foundation for bringing future technologies to life.

### Community impact

VLSI Wiki has become a valuable resource for:
- Engineering students learning semiconductor fundamentals
- Professional engineers seeking advanced technical knowledge
- Industry professionals staying updated with latest trends
- Career changers entering the semiconductor field

## Philosophy and vision

### Core questions and approach

Na's educational philosophy is driven by fundamental questions that many engineers face:

> "What does this term mean?"  
> "How should I choose my career path?"  
> "What if I make a mistake?"  
> "Who can I ask when I don't understand something?"  
> "And what about investing, debugging, or research papers...?"

These questions form the foundation of his approach to knowledge sharing and community building.

### Continuous improvement mindset

Na emphasizes continuous learning and improvement, stating: "No one starts off perfect, and once something is finished, it can help others—that in itself is a success." He actively seeks feedback from the community to enhance the quality and relevance of his content.

## Personal life

- **Nationality**: South Korean
- **Interests**: Movies, traveling, and exploring new technologies
- **Professional motto**: "죽을때까지 반도체를 연구합니다. 그리고 배운 지식을 공유합니다" ("I will research semiconductors until I die, and share the knowledge I learn")

### Work-life philosophy

Na maintains that VLSI Wiki is a side project, with his primary focus remaining on advancing the interests of his employer. He believes in providing valuable knowledge freely while hoping that the benefits others receive are "infinitely more valuable."
