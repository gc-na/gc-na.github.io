### Signal Integrity

**Definition:**
Signal Integrity refers to the quality of an electrical signal's transmission in an integrated circuit.

**Key Points:**
- Critical for reliable high-speed circuit operation
- Concerns both digital and analog signals
- Affected by various physical and electrical phenomena

**Main Issues:**
- Crosstalk
- Reflections
- Ringing
- Ground bounce
- Power supply noise

**Factors Affecting Signal Integrity:**
- Interconnect characteristics
- Switching speed
- Power distribution
- Layout and routing

**Mitigation Techniques:**
- Proper termination
- Controlled impedance routing
- Power/ground plane design
- Differential signaling

**Importance:**
- Ensures correct logic levels and timing
- Critical for meeting performance specifications
- Key consideration in high-speed and mixed-signal designs

Maintaining good signal integrity is essential for functionality and performance in modern VLSI designs.
