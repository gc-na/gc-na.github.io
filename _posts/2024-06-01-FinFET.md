### FinFET

**Definition:**
FinFET (Fin Field-Effect Transistor) is a 3D transistor structure where the channel is a thin, fin-like feature standing vertically from the substrate.

**Key Points:**
- Provides better electrostatic control over the channel
- Reduces short-channel effects
- Allows for continued scaling beyond planar CMOS limitations
- Used in advanced process nodes (22nm and below)

**Advantages:**
- Lower leakage current
- Higher drive current
- Improved sub-threshold slope
- Better scalability

**Challenges:**
- More complex manufacturing process
- Higher parasitic capacitance
- Self-heating effects

**Applications:**
- High-performance computing
- Mobile processors
- Advanced memory technologies

FinFETs are crucial for continuing Moore's Law in advanced semiconductor processes.
