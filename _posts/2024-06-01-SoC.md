### SoC (System on Chip)

**Definition:**
An SoC is an integrated circuit that incorporates most or all components of a computer or other electronic system into a single chip.

**Key Components:**
- Microprocessor or microcontroller
- Memory (RAM, ROM)
- Digital and analog interfaces
- Power management circuits
- Specialized processing units (GPU, DSP)

**Advantages:**
- Reduced power consumption
- Smaller form factor
- Lower overall cost in high volumes
- Improved performance

**Challenges:**
- Complex design and verification
- Higher initial development cost
- Heat management
- Integration of diverse IP blocks

**Applications:**
- Smartphones
- Embedded systems
- IoT devices
- Automotive electronics

SoCs are fundamental to modern electronic devices, enabling powerful, compact, and energy-efficient systems.
